About me

I'm a program manager and content strategist who's drawn to the challenge of making complex information clearer, more usable, and more human. Much of my work has focused on documentation and knowledge systems—optimizing not only the content but its structure, governenance, and maintainance so it can actually support the people who rely on it.

I enjoy working at the messy intersection of language, process, and systems. That means I alternate between editing and developing content, shaping editorial standards, or stepping back to look at how information flows across teams and programs. I'm especially interested in tranlating technical, regulatory, and policy requirements into content & communication that's accurate, accessible, and scalable.

While much of my professional work has been confidential, this portfolio highlights a small selection of public content and editing projects I've contributed to through volunteer work with nonprofit organizations. These examples reflect how I approach content development: with curiosity, care for detail, and a strong belief that clear, well‑designed information is a foundational part of an effective and equitable organization.
