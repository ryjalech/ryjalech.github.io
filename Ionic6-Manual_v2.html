User-Friendly Guide to Ioniq6 SE Car Settings

This guide provides user-friendly descriptions of the Ioniq6 SE features that you can configure through the central screen to modify the car's behavior. It is intended for Ioniq6 SE owners, to assist you with understanding the settings—what they do, what you can change about them, and where they are located in the car settings menu. This guide does not cover general driving controls, buttons, or settings outside the main central screen. This is not an authoritative or exhaustive guide; for example, it does not cover sound or navigation settings. This guide also does not cover other features in other Ioniq6 trims. The author is not responsible for inaccurate information. For the full list and descriptions, you should refer to the latest official manual from Hyundai.

Core Driving Features

The following settings affect primary driving features:

  1. Drive Modes

    The Ioniq6 comes with four preset Drive Modes: Eco, Normal, Sport, and Snow. Each drive mode differs in its configuration of acceleration strength, drive train, and regenerative breaking presets.

    Controls: You can replace Snow mode with customized settings. You can configure acceleration strength, drive train, and regenerative braking strength for Custom Mode.

    Setup > Vehicle > Drive Mode > Custom to switch between Snow and Custom. Select the gear icon in Custom Mode to customize its settings.
  2. Smart Regenerative Braking

    Your car's systems detect external conditions like speed and distance to other vehicles in front, then it automatically engages the brakes to maintain speed and distance. You can turn this feature on and off by holding the right paddle behind the steering wheel for five seconds.

    Controls: You can adjust the intensity of the Smart Regenerative Braking between Slow, Medium, and Strong.

    Setup > EV > Smart Regeneration System
  3. One-touch Turn Signal

    Pressing the turn signal lever up or down and releasing (not fully engaging) makes the signal blink a certain number of times.

    Controls: You can set the number of times the turn signal flashes on one-touch to 3, 5, or 7, or turn the feature off.

    Setup > Vehicle > Lights > One-touch Turn Signal
  4. High Beam Adjust

    When high beam is on and your car's system detects an oncoming car, this feature adjusts the headlights direction to reduce strain for oncoming drivers.

    Controls: You can toggle this feature on or off.

    Setup > Vehicle > Lights > High Beam Assist

Safety Features

The following settings assist with safe driving habits:

  1. Forward Collision Alert

    When your car's front sensors detect a fast-approaching object, the system engages the brakes, displays a warning, and plays an audible alert.

    Controls: You can toggle this feature on or off. You can also set the response timing of the warning to activate later (delay).

    Setup > Driver Assistance > Driving Safety > Forward Alert
  2. Proximity Sensor

    This feature detects objects close to your car when not in Park and stationary or moving slower than 6mph. On detection, the dashboard displays a color-coded indicator to show where the object is, and the car plays an audio alert: an object at 24–40 inches causes slow beeps & green indicator, at 12-24 inches causes fast beeps & yellow indicator, and at >12 inches causes a sustained tone & red indicator. (This feature is called the "Forward/Reverse Parking Distance Warning" in the manual.)

    Controls: You can toggle the Proximity Sensor to remain on. Otherwise, it automtically turns off once your car's speed exceeds 18mph.

    Setup > Vehicle > Parking Safety > Parking Distance Waning Auto On
  3. Blind Spot Sensor

    The Blind Spot Sensor detects when there is a vehicle in your blind spot and activates a warning light on the side mirrors. Engaging the turn signal while a vehicle is detected in the blind spot activates a beeping audio alert.

    Controls: You can toggled the Blind Spot Sensor on or off.

    Setup > Vehicle > Driver Assistance > Driving Safety > Blind-Spot Safety

    Controls: You can adjust the volume of the audio alert or select volume 0 to mute the audio alert.

    Location: Setup > Vehicle > Driving Assistance > Warning Methods
    **Note: The Driver Assistance Warning Methods settings control multiple features. Changing the haptic or volume settings of this feature will affect these other features as well: Rear Cross-Traffic Alert, Lane safety, and Safe Exit Alert.
  4. Rear-View Camera

    The Rear-View Camera displays the view from the rear-facing camera. The camera view displays on the central screen automatically when the car is put into Reverse, or you can turn it on manually by pressing the camera button below the central screen while stationary or moving at slow speeds.

    Controls: By default, the Rear-View camera turns off once you put your car into Drive (D) or Neutral (N). You can toggled it to stay on until your car's speed exceeds 6mph.

    Setup > Vehicle > Driver Assistance > Parking Safety > Camera Settings > Display Contents > Extended Rear View Monitor

    Controls: You can adjust the camera picture brightness and contrast.

    Setup > Vehicle > Diver Assistance > Parking Safety > Camera Settings > Display Settings

    Controls: You can set this feature to lower all other volume while the rear camera view is active.

    Location: Setup > Vehicle > Driving Assistance > Warning Methods > Parking Safety Priority
  5. Rear Cross-Traffic Alert

    When reversing your car and another vehicle approaches from the side, the Rear Cross-Traffic Alert will sound an audio alert, display red markers on the rear-view camera, and engage the steering wheel’s vibration (haptic response).

    Controls: You can toggle the Rear Cross-traffic Alert on or off.

    Setup > Vehicle > Parking Safety > Rear-Cross Traffic Safety

    Controls: You can adjust the volume of the audio alert or mute by selecting volume 0, and you can toggle the haptic response on or off.

    Setup > Driver Assistance > Warning Methods
    **Note: The Driver Assistance Warning Methods settings control multiple features. Changing the haptic or volume settings of this feature will affect these other features as well: Lane safety, Blind Spot Sensor, and Safe Exit Alert.
  6. Safe Exit Alert

    When your car is parked and a vehicle is approaching from the rear as any passenger opens their door, this feature will activate an audio alert and display a warning message on the dashboard.

    Controls: You can toggle this feature on or off.

    Setup > Driver Assistance > Driving Safety > Safe Exit

    Controls: You can adjust the volume of this alert. (You cannot mute this alert.)

    Setup > Driver Assistance > Warning Methods
    **Note: The Driver Assistance Warning Methods settings control multiple features. Changing the haptic or volume settings of this feature will affect these other features as well: Rear Cross-Traffic Alert, Lane safety, and Blind Spot Sensor.
  7. Lane Assistance

    Your car has two features to assist with staying within your lane while driving. Lane Keeping Assist plays a warning and makes small adjustments when sensors detect your car crossing lane lines. Lane Following Assist actively adjusts steering to keep your car within lane lines. Both features can be activated independently or simultaneously with the button on the steering wheel by pressing once (LKA) or holding (LFA).

    Controls: You can toggle Lane Keeping Assist on or off in the Settings.

    Setup > Driver Assistance > Driving Safety > Lane Safety

    Controls: You can adjust the volume of the audio alert.

    Setup > Driver Assistance > Warning Methods
    **Note: The Driver Assistance Warning Methods settings control multiple features. Changing the haptic or volume settings of this feature will affect these other features as well: Rear Cross-Traffic Alert, Blind Spot Sensor, and Safe Exit Alert.

Convenience and Comfort Features

The following features offer helpful alerts or assist with internal comfort:

  1. Leading Car Departure

    This feature plays a beeping alert and displays a message on the dashboard console to notify the driver when the car in front has pulled away.

    Controls: You can toggle the Leading Car Departure feature on or off.

    Setup > Driver Assistance > Driver Attention Warning
  2. Traffic Camera Alert

    The Traffic Camera Alert plays an audio message when the navigation system detects an upcoming traffic camera, such as a speed camera or traffic light camera.

    Controls: You can configure the alert to activate relative to your car's speed, or to activate at different distances from the traffic camera.
    **Note: During latest testing as of April 2025, this feature's controls failed to load. The author will update this content if they are able to resolve the error.**

    Setup > Navigation > Alerts > Camera Alert Distance

    Controls: You can adjust the alert volume, or you can silence it by setting the volume to 0.

    Setup > Sound > Navigation > Navigation Alerts
    **Note: The Navigation Alert volume affects all Navigation Alerts. Changing the volume of this feature will affect other navigation features.
  3. Speed Limit Detection

    This feature displays the posted speed on the dashboard using navigation data.

    Controls: You can toggle this feature on and off. (There are two options shown in the feature settings, but during real-world testing at low speeds there were no noticeable differences between the two.)

    Setup > Vehicle > Driver Assistance > Speed Limit
  4. Smart Cruise Control & Highway Driving Assist

    Smart Cruise control (SCC) detects the car in front and automatically sets the cruise control speed to maintain distance. Highway Driving Assist assists with keeping the car within the lane lines.

    Controls: You can toggle SCC on or off, and you can adjsut the distance.

    Setup > Driver Assist > Convenience > Smart Cruise Control

    Controls: You can toggle Highway Driving Assist on or off.

    Setup > Driver Assist > Convenience > Highway Driving
  5. Headlight Auto-shutoff

    When you turn off the car or put it into ACC mode, the headlights will automatically turn off after you open and close your door, or after 5 minutes of no activity.

    Controls: You can set the headlights to stay on for 15 seconds after opening and closing your door. This feature also causes the lights to turn on when you unlock the doors with the key fob while headlights are in the AUTO position and the sensors detect darkness outside.

    Setup > Vehicle > Lights > Headlight Delay
  6. Auto Unlock Doors from Outside

    When you approach the car while carrying the key fob, the car doors unlock automatically.

    Controls: You can toggle this feature on or off.

    Setup > Vehicle > Door > Approach Unlock
  7. Auto Lock and Unlock Doors Driving

    Doors automatically lock or unlock based on your car's activity or driving conditions.

    Controls: You can set the car to lock its doors when you shift out of park or when car's speed exceeds 9mph.

    Setup > Vehicle > Door > Auto Lock

    Controls: You can set the car to automatically unlock its doors when you shift into park, when you turn off the car, or disable auto unlock.

    Location: Setup > Vehicle > Door > Auto Unlock
  8. Auto-Open Trunk

    When you approach the rear of your car while carrying the key fob, the trunk opens automatically.

    Controls: You can toggle this feature on or off.

    Setup > Vehicle > Door > Smart Trunk
  9. Recirculate Air Auto-shutoff

    When Climate controls are set to recirculate the air, after five minutes the system will automatically return controls to fresh air to help regulate humidity.

    Controls: You can toggle this feature on or off.

    Setup > Vehicle > Climate > Automatic ventilation – Auto dehumidifier
  10. Recirculate Air When Using Wiper Fluid

    When you spray the windshield wiper fluid, the system will automatically switch the climate controls to Recirculate Air to prevent the windshield fluid odors from blowing into the car.

    Controls: You can toggle this feature on or off.

    Setup > Vehicle > Climate > Recirculate Air
  11. Rear Occupant Alert

    When the system detects weight in the backseat after you turn off your car and open the driver door, this feature notifies you with a warning on the dashboard. You can acknowledge and close the warning by pressing the OK button.

    Controls: You can toggle this feature on or off.

    Setup > Vehicle > Convenience > Rear Occupant Alert
  12. Screen Settings: Dark Theme and Blue Light Filter

    You can modify the lighting and coloration themes of the central screen and dashboard settings to suit your comfort. Both screens are linked to the same settings; you cannot adjust them independently.

    Controls: You can set the screens to White theme or Black theme. You can also set Black theme to turn on automatically either based off lighting conditions or based off a schedule you set.

    Setup > Screen Theme/Layout > Graphic Themes

    Controls: You can adjust the brightness and blue light filter intensity. You can also set the blue light filter to turn on automatically based off lighting conditions or based off a schedule you set.

    Setup > Display > Illumination / Blue Light Filter OR Setup > Vehicle > Cluster > Illumination > Illumination / Blue Light Filter
